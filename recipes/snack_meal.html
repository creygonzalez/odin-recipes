Eating Snacks Until It's a Meal

Introduction

If you're anything like me, there are some days where cooking a meal is the absolute LAST thing you want to do after a long day

Instead, I like to do what I call, "Eating little bits of whatever I can find until the calories equate to a meal, or I'm full".

If that feels too said, call it a makeshift charcuterie board without the board, presentation, or delicious assortment of meats, cheeses, crackers, and fruit.

In this recipe, if you can call it that, we'll take a look at some of our options, as well as substitutions


Ingredients

Now, if you're anything like me, you live in what's known as an ingredients household

This just means that I don't keep around Doritos, or Fruit Roll Ups, or anything fun for that matter.

I'll take you through what I have in my pantry at this moment in time, and we'll make a meal out of it

  • Handful of salted peanuts
  • Bread
  • Peanut butter or jelly
  • Rice pudding
  • Apples, although any fresh-ish fruit is okay
  • Cheese
  • Deli meat
  • Pickles
  • Popcorn

Instructions

There isn't anything specific that's needed to be done here for prep. If you've got 10 minutes and a mildly stocked pantry, you're good to go

  1. Put your bread in a toaster
  2. While the bread is toasting, wrap the cheese in the meat. You can also eat them separately
  3. Eat the apple or fresh fruit
  4. Devour a few pickles. My favorite are butter pickles
  5. Might as well throw the popcorn in the microwave too
  6. Oh! The toast is done. Time to spread your condiment of choice (within reason please)
  7. Now that you've eaten everything else, time for dessert. Tonight for me, it's popcorn and rice pudding

And that's it! Super simple meal with no prep or thought involved. This is best served with a YouTube video about your favorite topic.

Thanks for coming with me on this journey!
